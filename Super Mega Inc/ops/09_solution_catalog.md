# Solution Catalog

This is the real SuperMega product ladder.

Rule:
- sell small working systems
- do not sell a giant generic platform
- every product needs a named buyer, a setup path, and a queue owner

## Core products now

### Distributor Sales Desk
- Buyer: owner-led distributors, importers, commercial managers
- Replaces: scattered Facebook leads, Excel lists, Viber follow-up, manual reminders
- Promise: find companies, clean lists, draft outreach, and keep the next step visible
- Daily users: founder, sales lead, sales operator
- Setup path:
  - import current lead list
  - connect outbound follow-up flow
  - create one sales queue
  - enable Founder Brief
- Expands to:
  - quote follow-up
  - deal review
  - contact intake to pipeline

### List Cleanup Desk
- Buyer: sales coordinators, admin teams, assistants, founders
- Replaces: messy exports from Facebook, Google, Viber, WhatsApp, Excel, CRM
- Promise: turn raw company names into one usable working list with owner and next step
- Daily users: sales/admin operators
- Setup path:
  - paste or upload current list
  - normalize rows
  - assign owner and stage
  - push into the sales queue
- Expands to:
  - list dedupe
  - contact enrichment
  - campaign batching

### Receiving Control
- Buyer: warehouse managers, procurement leads, plant operators
- Replaces: paper logs, chat messages, GRN mismatch confusion, missed supplier follow-up
- Promise: log receiving issues once and keep shortages, holds, and next actions visible
- Daily users: receiving clerk, manager, buyer
- Setup path:
  - define issue reasons
  - capture current inbound issue flow
  - create one receiving queue
  - enable exception review
- Expands to:
  - approval flow
  - supplier scorecards
  - daily receiving brief

### Founder Brief
- Buyer: founder, GM, director
- Replaces: manual check-ins across chat, spreadsheets, and hallway follow-up
- Promise: one short daily view of what is blocked, overdue, or waiting for approval
- Daily users: founder, GM
- Setup path:
  - connect live queues
  - define priority rules
  - schedule daily brief
- Expands to:
  - weekly business review
  - incident escalation
  - release/status review

## Productized systems next

### Approval Flow
- Buyer: finance, procurement, operations managers
- Replaces: message-based approvals and missing sign-off history
- Promise: one approval queue with timestamps, owner, and decision history
- Starts from:
  - purchase approvals
  - exception approvals
  - rollout approvals

### Order Inbox Desk
- Buyer: distributors, sellers, service teams, restaurants
- Replaces: orders scattered across Facebook, Viber, WhatsApp, phone, and paper
- Promise: collect inbound orders into one queue with owner, status, and next action
- Starts from:
  - message order capture
  - phone order capture
  - simple order board
- Expands to:
  - QR ordering
  - customer notifications
  - back-office fulfillment

### QR Ordering Desk
- Buyer: restaurants, cafes, food halls, service counters
- Replaces: paper menu friction, manual order relays, missing order history
- Promise: scan, order, route to queue, and keep service status visible
- Starts from:
  - menu
  - table code or counter code
  - kitchen/service queue
- Expands to:
  - payment integration
  - loyalty
  - inventory tie-in

### Client Portal Starter
- Buyer: agencies, service firms, project operators, B2B account teams
- Replaces: status updates buried in email and chat
- Promise: one client-facing place for status, files, approvals, and next step
- Starts from:
  - status board
  - approvals
  - document drop
- Expands to:
  - ticketing
  - billing status
  - account review

### Document Intake
- Buyer: back office, operations, procurement, finance
- Replaces: email attachments and untracked document handling
- Promise: collect documents, classify them, and assign the next action
- Starts from:
  - invoice intake
  - PO intake
  - supplier document intake

## Productized systems later

### Commerce Back Office
- Buyer: retailers, importers, D2C operators
- Replaces: brittle plugin stacks and manual ops checks
- Promise: one operating layer for catalog, orders, exceptions, and follow-up
- Build bias:
  - Shopify storefront when a merchant already uses Shopify
  - Medusa or Vendure when custom operational control matters more than theme tooling

### Learning Hub
- Buyer: companies onboarding staff, training channel partners, or rolling out SOPs
- Replaces: static PDFs, chat-based training, and untracked completion
- Promise: guided training with progress, checkpoints, and manager visibility

## What we do not sell first

- giant generic ERP
- giant browser-first autonomous agent platform
- broad "AI transformation" retainers with no named workflow
- custom software with no starter queue or operating owner
