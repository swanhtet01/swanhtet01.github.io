# Founder Brand And LinkedIn

This document defines the founder-facing brand for SuperMega.

## Founder identity

Use:
- `Swan Htet`
- `Founder, SuperMega`

Do not use:
- stacked buzzword titles
- inflated claims
- language that sounds bigger than the actual systems

## Company line

Primary line:
- `SuperMega builds systems for sales, operations, client work, and founder review.`

Short line:
- `Systems for real company work.`

Website line:
- `supermega.dev shows the systems. app.supermega.dev runs the work.`

## Founder voice

Sound like:
- clear
- practical
- operator-minded
- implementation-first

Avoid:
- hype
- vague AI claims
- generic software-agency language

## In-person introduction

Use:

`I build systems for sales, operations, client work, and founder review. SuperMega turns messy workflows into one clean operating surface.`

Shorter version:

`I build operating systems for real company work.`

## LinkedIn headline

Recommended:

`Founder at SuperMega | Systems for sales, operations, client work, and founder review`

Alternative:

`Founder at SuperMega | Building operating systems for real company work`

## LinkedIn about

Recommended version:

`I build systems for real company work.`

`At SuperMega, we design and ship systems for sales, operations, client work, and founder review. The goal is simple: replace scattered chats, spreadsheets, and disconnected tools with one clean surface the team can actually run from.`

`Current focus: owner-led distributors, operators, warehouses, service teams, and founders who need clearer daily execution.`

## Public positioning

Lead with:
- systems
- examples
- contact

Support with:
- live demos
- one clear workflow at a time
- proof of the operating surface

Do not lead with:
- frameworks
- internal agent architecture
- generic "we can build anything" language

## Business card rule

The card should do one job:
- get the person to `https://supermega.dev`

Front:
- founder
- company
- one short company line

Back:
- one strong QR
- the site URL
- one short instruction

## Messaging guardrails

Prefer:
- sales systems
- operations systems
- client portals
- founder brief
- internal operating workflows

Avoid:
- `AI platform`
- `ERP replacement`
- `super app`
- `we do everything`

## Public proof rule

The founder brand should point people to:
- a small number of strong systems
- real examples
- one contact path

That is stronger than a long capability list.
