# SuperMega Myanmar Reset

## Blunt Assessment

The current project has been trying to sell too many things at once:

- a public website
- a lead finder
- a workspace
- an ERP layer
- a multi-agent operating system
- internal delivery tools

That spread is why it feels fake. The user cannot tell what to do first, and the company cannot tell what to sell first.

## What To Stop Doing

Stop trying to sell these as public products right now:

- generic AI OS for everything
- public ERP suite
- multi-agent platform as a front-door offer
- too many public routes and pages
- feature-first marketing copy

Stop building anything that requires the buyer to understand a taxonomy before getting value.

## What To Sell First

Sell only three things.

### 1. Lead Finder

Promise:
- find real businesses
- keep the shortlist
- draft the first outreach

Why it wins over Google or Facebook:
- Google gives pages, not a working shortlist
- Facebook gives profiles, not a usable follow-up system
- Lead Finder should do search + shortlist + outreach + first task in one loop

Target buyers:
- founders
- distributors
- agencies
- sales teams

### 2. Action OS Starter

Promise:
- one queue for follow-up, blockers, and daily next steps

Input:
- pasted team updates
- pasted lead lists
- one-off ops issues

Output:
- one clear queue
- owner
- due date
- next step

Target buyers:
- owner-led SMEs
- GM / operations managers
- sales managers

### 3. Myanmar Ops Control

This is the local wedge for ERP-style customers.

Promise:
- capture receiving, stock, quality, and payment issues without a full ERP rollout

Start with:
- receiving issue intake
- stock or availability issue queue
- overdue payment follow-up queue

Target buyers:
- importers
- distributors
- factories
- warehouses
- trading companies

## Myanmar-Specific Reasoning

Myanmar buyers are more likely to trust software that:

- starts from WhatsApp, Viber, Gmail, Sheets, and pasted notes
- works with weak process maturity
- does not demand a heavy ERP migration first
- shows a visible owner and next action immediately

The right motion is not “replace SAP”.
The right motion is:

- give them one queue
- give them one follow-up loop
- give them one place to keep context

## Public Product Flow

Keep the public flow to three pages:

1. `/`
   Explain the simplest entry points.
2. `/lead-finder`
   Search and save leads.
3. `/workspace`
   Start from a lead list, messy updates, or one ops issue.

Everything else should redirect or live in the private app.

## Private App Flow

The private app should be for actual operating work, not discovery.

Keep the main private screens to:

- `Sales`
- `Queue`
- `Issues`
- `Approvals`
- `Director`

Everything else is secondary.

## Agent Company Model

The agent company should not be “many smart bots”.
It should be a few durable loops.

### Growth Agent

Owns:
- saved lead hunts
- shortlist quality
- first outreach draft
- follow-up reminders

Human monitors:
- reply quality
- target market quality
- conversion

### Queue Agent

Owns:
- turning pasted updates into queue rows
- deduping tasks
- suggesting owner and due date
- aging and escalation

Human monitors:
- queue accuracy
- owner mapping
- overdue items

### Ops Agent

Owns:
- receiving issue intake
- stock or supplier issue intake
- quality or payment issue tagging
- issue-to-queue conversion

Human monitors:
- issue severity
- actual plant/commercial outcome

### Director Agent

Owns:
- daily brief
- top blockers
- overdue items
- high-risk exceptions

Human monitors:
- decision quality
- escalation thresholds

## What The Agents Must Not Do

Do not let agents hide the work.

Every agent output must become one of:

- a lead
- a queue item
- an issue
- an approval
- a director brief

If the output is not visible and reviewable in one of those places, it is not product value.

## Immediate Product Priorities

### Priority 1

Make public Workspace the default usable entry for any company:

- import lead list
- import messy updates
- import ops issues
- generate first queue

### Priority 2

Make Lead Finder the internal and external wedge:

- internal: use it to find SuperMega clients
- external: sell it as a product

### Priority 3

Make shared cloud persistence the default, not browser-local fallback.

### Priority 4

Require recoverable identity on first shared save:

- email
- company
- workspace name

## Company Priorities

The company is still not operationally ready.

Build next:

- onboarding checklist
- pricing
- billing
- support inbox
- telemetry
- CRM for SuperMega's own pipeline
- customer delivery SOP

## Final Principle

Do not build more categories.

Build tighter loops:

- find
- save
- queue
- follow up
- review

That is the reset.
